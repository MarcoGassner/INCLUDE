The INCLUDE (INterCuLtUral Child Development) project integrates clinical diagnostics and therapeutic care with research by providing low-threshold services for children and families with refugee experiences, accompanied by continuous scientific evaluation. In addition, we aim to adapt and develop diagnostic, assessment, and therapeutic methods to be universally applicable and culturally sensitive. The INCLUDE team’s current research focuses on vulnerable populations with experiences of forced migration, as well as children and parents living in active war zones. Central to our work is the promotion of healthy child development in interaction with the respective social environment. To achieve this, we investigate developmental risks associated with high levels of stress and deprivation in children and their caregivers, while also identifying and treating post-traumatic stress and adjustment disorders.
